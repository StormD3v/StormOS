# Content Strategy and Information Architecture

## Overview

The StormOS content strategy ensures all content serves the emotional journey, communicates Storm's value proposition, and provides clear pathways for different audiences. This document specifies content organization, messaging frameworks, and information architecture principles.

## Content Philosophy

### Core Principles

1. **Show, Don't Just Tell:** Demonstrate capabilities through projects and code, not just claims
2. **Quality Over Quantity:** Fewer, higher-quality pieces of content
3. **Audience-Centric:** Tailor content for each target audience
4. **Story-Driven:** Use narrative to engage and inspire
5. **Action-Oriented:** Every piece of content guides toward an action
6. **Authentic Voice:** Maintain Storm's genuine voice throughout

### Content Goals

**Primary Goals:**
- Establish Storm as a credible software engineer
- Demonstrate technical depth and capability
- Generate excitement about WorldForge
- Facilitate meaningful connections

**Secondary Goals:**
- Share knowledge and insights
- Build community around shared interests
- Document Storm's journey and growth

## Content Guidelines

### Writing Style

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Professional but approachable
- Confident but not arrogant
- Technical but accessible
- Authentic and genuine

**Writing Principles:**
- Use active voice
- Be concise and clear
- Avoid jargon unless necessary
- Explain technical concepts simply
- Use examples and analogies
- Show, don't just tell

### Tone Guidelines

**Appropriate Tone:**
- Professional credibility
- Technical competence
- Creative thinking
- Authentic personality

**Inappropriate Tone:**
- Overly casual
- Excessively formal
- Arrogant or boastful
- Overly technical without explanation

### Content Length

**Guidelines:**
- Homepage sections: 2-4 paragraphs each
- About page: 500-800 words total
- Project descriptions: 200-400 words
- Blog posts: 800-2000 words
- Vision statements: 300-500 words

**Rationale:**
- Respect user's time
- Provide sufficient depth
- Maintain engagement
- Optimize for readability

## Content Performance

### Metrics to Track

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Time on page
- Scroll depth
- Bounce rate
- Pages per session

**Content Metrics:**
- Most viewed pages
- Most engaged sections
- Exit pages
- Conversion funnels

**Audience Metrics:**
- Traffic sources
- Geographic distribution
- Device usage
- Return visitor rate

### Success Criteria

**Homepage:**
- Time on page: > 90 seconds
- Scroll depth: > 60% reach Projects section
- Bounce rate: < 50%

**Projects:**
- Project modal opens: > 30% of visitors
- Multiple project views: > 15% view 3+ projects
- GitHub click-through: > 15%

**WorldForge:**
- Section engagement: > 50% scroll to bottom
- Waitlist signup: > 10% of section visitors

**Blog:**
- Article reads: > 15% read at least one article
- Average read time: > 3 minutes
- Social shares: > 5% share articles
