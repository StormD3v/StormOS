# VOICE.md — StormD3v

This document is the permanent source of truth for every design decision, UI interaction, piece of copy, and motion effect on the StormD3v website. It supersedes intuition. It answers questions before they are asked. When something feels uncertain, come here first.

---

## 1. What StormD3v Is

StormD3v is a studio. Not an agency, not a portfolio, not a startup.

It is the long-term home of ambitious work, built by Friday Gift Chinecherem Azunda. The work here is not produced on a sprint cycle and shipped before it is ready. It is given the time it needs. Some things take years. That is the point.

StormD3v exists because certain ideas deserve more than a weekend. The kind of ideas that compound over time, that get better the longer you stay with them, that could not have been rushed into existence without losing something important. StormD3v is the place where those ideas are taken seriously.

It is not a company with a roadmap. It is not a portfolio with metrics. It is not a SaaS product with a pricing page. It is a workspace. Visitors are entering somewhere, not browsing something.

The studio currently contains two products: WorldForge (Project: Isekai) and LumiCast (Project: Real Weather). WorldForge is the flagship, receiving the majority of focus and presence. LumiCast is available and real, but quieter. More will come in time, without announcement until they are ready.

StormOS is the philosophy behind the work — the operating system for how Friday Gift thinks about building. It is not a product, not a brand, not a navigation item. It is the atmosphere the work carries.

## 2. What Visitors Should Feel

The homepage is not a pitch. It is a journey.

A visitor arrives and feels **curiosity** — the name and the atmosphere, nothing explained yet.

They scroll and feel **interest** — the work is visible, and there is more to it than a quick read reveals.

They reach WorldForge and feel **wonder** — this is something serious, something alive, something they did not expect to encounter here.

They reach the About section and feel **trust** — there is a real person behind this, with a clear reason for building, no credentials required.

They reach Contact and feel **connection** — an invitation, not a sales pitch. Simple. Direct. Unhurried.

The site reveals itself gradually. It does not present everything at once. Each section earns the next. Visitors should leave with more questions than they arrived with, because the questions are the right kind — the kind that make them want to return.

Never overwhelm. Never attempt to impress. Let discovery happen naturally.

## 3. Voice

StormD3v speaks the way a person does when they are confident and not trying to prove it.

The voice is **calm**. It does not raise itself. It does not perform. When something is worth saying, it says it plainly, and that is enough.

The voice is **precise**. Every word is chosen. Nothing is filler. A sentence that does not earn its place is removed.

The voice is **human**. It sounds like one thoughtful person wrote it, because that is what happened. It does not sound like a marketing team. It does not sound like an AI prompt. It does not sound like a template.

The voice is **welcoming**, but it does not over-explain. It trusts the visitor to be intelligent. It leaves room for their own interpretation.

The voice carries **depth without performance**. Mystery should arise naturally from genuine depth, not from withholding information for stylistic effect. If something is worth saying, say enough of it. Leave the rest for the person to find when they go deeper.

### Words That Are Banned

These words do not belong here. Not in copy, not in metadata, not in button labels, not in alt text.

- revolutionary
- cutting-edge
- innovative
- powerful
- next generation
- ecosystem
- premium
- solution
- platform
- empowering creators
- state-of-the-art
- AI-powered
- seamless
- robust
- world-class
- game-changing

If a sentence requires one of these words to make its point, the sentence has not made its point. Rewrite it.

### What Startup Language Sounds Like

Startup language inflates. It promises scale, disruption, transformation. It speaks to investors, not to people.

StormD3v does not do this. The work is real. The copy reflects the work, not a version of it that sounds more impressive. If something is still being built, say it is still being built. If something is uncertain, leave it open. Authenticity is more interesting than polish.

## 4. Copy Rules

Every sentence on the site must serve at least one of these five purposes:

1. Reveal character
2. Create curiosity
3. Build trust
4. Explain purpose
5. Reinforce identity

If a sentence does none of these, it does not belong on the site. Remove it.

### Mechanics

Do not use em dashes. Use a comma, a period, or a new sentence.

Do not split a single thought across five one-sentence paragraphs. Write prose. Let ideas connect. Fragmentation is not the same as restraint.

Do not explain everything. Answer enough that the visitor understands. Leave enough that they want to continue.

Do not over-punctuate. Sentences breathe on their own. Trust them.

Prefer present tense. Prefer active voice. Prefer specific over general.

When writing about WorldForge, write from experience, not specification. Write about what it feels like to exist inside it, not what it is capable of doing.

When writing about StormD3v, write about why it exists, not what it produces.

When writing about Friday Gift, write about the reason, not the résumé.

### A Test for Every Sentence

Before publishing any copy, read the sentence aloud. Ask: does this sound like a person who knows what they are doing and does not need to convince anyone of that? If it sounds like a brochure, rewrite it.

## 5. WorldForge

WorldForge is not software being demonstrated. WorldForge is a place being described.

The distinction matters for every word written about it.

Software has features. A place has texture. People do not interact with a place's features — they live in it, they remember it, they are changed by it.

When copy describes WorldForge, it should describe: identity, history, legacy, belonging, permanence, memory, consequence, living worlds. These are not metaphors layered on top of a product. They are what WorldForge actually is.

People who enter WorldForge leave legacies. Their decisions accumulate into history. There are no resets. This is not a tagline — it is the design of the world. Copy that describes it should feel like that is already true.

WorldForge should make a visitor curious through authenticity, not through deliberate obscurity. Answer enough that they understand the vision. Leave enough that they want to be part of it.

The First Crossing is the name for the initial group of people who will enter WorldForge before it opens publicly. That name should carry weight. It is not a beta program. It is the beginning of a world's history.

## 6. Motion

Motion on this site communicates one thing at a time. It does not decorate. It does not perform.

**Every animation has a job.** An entrance animation opens a section. A transition moves between states. An atmospheric animation reinforces the feeling of a space. If an animation does none of these, it is removed.

**Nothing bounces.** Nothing spins. Nothing pulses for the sake of energy. The interface is composed, not playful.

**Speed is restraint.** Fast animations feel nervous. The pace here is deliberate. Section entrances take at least 400ms. The arrival overlay takes roughly 1.5 seconds. Nothing rushes.

**The arrival overlay is not a loading screen.** It is the visitor crossing a threshold. The site already exists beneath it. The overlay introduces the space. When it lifts, the visitor is already home.

**Reduced motion is respected.** When a visitor has indicated a preference for reduced motion, the emotional journey is communicated entirely through typography and spacing. The meaning survives without the motion. It was never dependent on it.

## 7. Interface Philosophy

The interface should feel like entering someone's workspace.

Not their portfolio. Not their dashboard. Their workspace — where real work happens, where tools are where they belong, where there is a sense that someone was just here.

Inspirations include: the atmosphere of Solo Leveling's Status Window, Iron Man's holographic workshop, Arcane's laboratories, cinematic operating systems, premium game interfaces. Not their appearance. Their feeling.

These interfaces share something: they feel **inhabited**. They feel like they were made for a specific person doing specific work. They are confident without being loud. They have depth without overwhelming.

Do not imitate their aesthetic. Capture what they make you feel.

### What This Means in Practice

- **Whitespace is space, not emptiness.** It is the room a workspace needs to breathe.
- **Typography does the heavy lifting.** Size, weight, and contrast carry meaning that decoration would obscure.
- **Borders separate things that should be separated.** If things do not need separation, whitespace is enough.
- **Colour is used purposefully.** Purple belongs to WorldForge. Neutral belongs to everything else. There is no third category.
- **Every element was placed there.** Nothing drifted in. If a visitor looks at something and wonders why it is there, it should not be there.

---

## 8. Design Principles

**Whitespace over borders.** Empty space is a design decision, not an absence of one.

**Typography over decoration.** If the hierarchy is clear, decoration has nothing to add. If it is not clear, decoration will not fix it.

**Lighting over gradients.** Ambient glows are atmosphere. Hard gradients are wallpaper.

**Depth over clutter.** A few things that feel substantial are better than many things that feel thin.

**Purpose over trends.** If a trend does not serve the identity of this site, it does not belong here, regardless of how common it is elsewhere.

**Identity over aesthetics.** The site should feel unmistakably like StormD3v. A design choice that could belong on any site does not belong here.

**Consistency over novelty.** The same decisions, made the same way, across every page. That consistency is what makes the identity feel real.

### The Governing Question

Before any element is added, any copy is written, any animation is designed, ask:

**Does this make StormD3v more memorable?**

If the answer is no, remove it. If the answer is uncertain, the answer is no.

## 9. The Arrival

The arrival animation lasts roughly 1.5 seconds.

During that time, the visitor crosses a threshold. The page begins almost black. A faint purple atmosphere becomes visible. Particles drift slowly through the dark. The name StormD3v fades in at scale. A soft electrical pulse travels through the letters, left to right, once. Below it, quietly: "Built by StormD3v." Then the overlay lifts and the site is there, already waiting.

The visitor never left the page. The overlay did not load the site — the site was already loaded. The overlay introduced it.

There is no loading bar. There is no skip button. The sequence is short enough that neither is needed. The experience is the point.

---

## 10. Before Writing or Building Anything

This applies to UI, copy, interactions, and animations equally.

Read what you are about to create. Then ask:

1. Does this make StormD3v more memorable?
2. Does this sound or feel like a person, or like a template?
3. Does this serve a purpose that can be named?
4. Would someone return to this site because of this?

If the honest answer to question 1 is no, stop. Rewrite it. Redesign it. Remove it. The goal is not to finish quickly. The goal is to build something worth remembering.
